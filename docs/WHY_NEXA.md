# Why Nexa-next (honest positioning)

Nexa-next targets operators who want **most of the “AI employee” story** without surrendering data, budget, or execution control.

## Where Nexa tends to win

- **Privacy posture**: Redaction, strict modes, and egress policies are part of the architecture—not optional extras.
- **Cost transparency**: Token economy hooks surface budgets and blocks before invoices surprise you.
- **Execution governance**: Workspace roots, approvals, and Mission Control give humans leverage over autonomous loops.
- **Local-first options**: Ollama and other OpenAI-compatible local endpoints are first-class in routing—not demos.

## Where Nexa is still catching up

- **Ecosystem size**: OpenClaw-like skill marketplaces are larger today. Nexa’s marketplace MVP is local, validated, and conservative by design.
- **Voice + mobile polish**: Telegram is solid; voice transcription is feature-flagged until local ASR lands by default.
- **Sandbox isolation**: True hardware-backed isolation is roadmap work—today’s sandbox module surfaces effective mode honestly.

## Who should look elsewhere

Teams that need **vendor-delivered SOC 2 guarantees**, fully managed global fleets, or unrestricted third-party tool egress without policy work should expect to add external controls—or choose a different product.

Nexa-next is for builders who want **magic with enforcement knobs**, not magic with blind trust.
